# Daisy 1.0.7.10 — reliable capture, a livelier flower, and smarter speakers

## Fixed

- Recording reliably picks up your voice. Daisy captures the microphone at a lower level (Core Audio), fixing cases where a recording came back empty or missed your side — most often the built-in mic or a wired headset on macOS 26, where the audio engine could lock onto the wrong sample rate and capture nothing.
- The recording flower is alive again. Its petals move with your voice while you record — under heavy live transcription they could sit frozen even though audio and the transcript were flowing; now they track the live spectrum.

## Added

- Daisy can recognize people by their calendar email, not just their voice. When a recording is tied to a calendar meeting, Daisy matches an attendee to a speaker you've already named — by their invite address — so they're still recognized on a day their voice sounds different (a new mic, a cold, speakerphone).
- A new "Match known speakers" control in Settings → Speakers, with three modes. **Automatic** (the default, and how Daisy has always worked) labels a recognized person as soon as a recording finishes. **Suggest** recognizes them but waits — the name appears as a suggestion in the recording's "Name the speakers" card, and you confirm before it's applied. **Off** turns cross-meeting auto-labeling off entirely; speakers stay "Remote A / B" until you name them (fingerprints are still saved, so you can switch it back on anytime).
- A speaker editor. Open any known speaker in Settings to add their email addresses (so calendar invites match them) and notes — role, company, how you met — or forget them. It also lists every recording where you've named them, so you can jump straight to a past meeting.

## Changed

- Smoother capture under load. The microphone's disk writing and audio analysis run off the real-time audio thread, so heavy live transcription competes less with capture — fewer glitches on busy machines.
- Each person in the Known speakers list now opens a detail card when clicked.
- The "only your voice" notice (shown when Screen Recording is off) is now a single tap that opens the permission settings.

## Notes

- Your existing speakers keep working unchanged: the new fields start empty, the new setting defaults to Automatic, and voice fingerprints, emails, and notes all stay on your Mac.
