# Daisy 1.0.7.10 — speaker recognition that works on your terms

## Added

- Daisy can now recognize people by their calendar email, not just their voice. When a recording is tied to a calendar meeting, Daisy matches an attendee to a speaker you've already named — by their invite address — so they're still recognized on a day their voice sounds different (a new mic, a cold, speakerphone).
- A new "Match known speakers" control in Settings → Speakers, with three modes. **Automatic** (the default, and how Daisy has always worked) labels a recognized person as soon as a recording finishes. **Suggest** recognizes them but waits — the name appears as a suggestion in the recording's "Name the speakers" card, and you confirm before it's applied. **Off** turns cross-meeting auto-labeling off entirely; speakers stay "Remote A / B" until you name them (your voice fingerprints are still saved, so you can switch it back on anytime).
- A speaker editor. Open any known speaker in Settings to add their email addresses (so calendar invites match them) and notes — role, company, how you met — or forget them.

## Changed

- Each person in the Known speakers list now opens a detail card when clicked, instead of only offering Forget.

## Notes

- Your existing speakers keep working unchanged: the new fields start empty, the new setting defaults to Automatic, and voice fingerprints, emails, and notes all stay on your Mac.
