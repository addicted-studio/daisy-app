# Daisy 1.0.7.19

- Auto-stop got a brain transplant. It now reliably notices when a meeting is over: the "is anyone still talking" check no longer gets stuck on a frozen system-audio level or tripped by keyboard clicks and fan noise (both could keep a finished meeting recording for an extra half-hour). It also stops after ~10 minutes of total silence even before the scheduled end — the "everyone left early" case.
- New: "Ask before auto-stopping" (Settings → Recording). Instead of stopping on its own, Daisy sends a notification — Stop & save, 10 more minutes, or 30 more minutes.
- Meetings auto-started from your calendar now take the event's title instead of a "Meeting 2026-06-12 12:31" placeholder.
- Dictation is snappier on release: the model is pre-warmed at launch, the final decode uses a lighter profile tuned for dictation, and the throwaway live pass no longer delays the paste.
- New in Settings → Transcription: "Live preview while dictating" — an experimental streaming engine shows words ~0.6 s behind your speech while you hold the key (the pasted text is still produced by the main engine).
- Model downloads are finally visible: the main window and the menu-bar popover now show a progress banner — checking, downloading with a percent, loading — instead of hiding it in a tooltip.
- The Record button is green when idle. Orange now always means "the mic is (about to be) live" — pause/resume, the widget, the warnings — so a parked app no longer looks like it's recording.
- Recording shortcuts: assigning a key combo while two recorder fields were active could write the combo to the wrong action (e.g. Dictation's combo landing on Voice note). Only one recorder listens at a time now.
- The elapsed timer moved into the record button in the popover; the redundant status caption and pulsing dot are gone.
- ⌘R (View → Refresh Meetings) re-reads your calendar on demand — no more waiting for the next automatic tick after adding an event.
- Library search is much faster on large libraries (token-index prefilter; same results).
- Help → Send Log Report: collects the last 24 h of Daisy's logs plus an environment summary and opens a pre-addressed email — you review it and press Send. For when something misbehaves and you want us to actually see why.
- Settings → Summary now tells you exactly why a provider is unavailable instead of a bare "Unavailable". For Apple Intelligence it names the actual cause — not turned on, still downloading, this Mac isn't eligible, or (a common one) your Mac and Siri are set to different languages — with what to do about it.
- Privacy, enforced in code: the on-device audio engines (speaker diarization, voice detection, the Parakeet dictation engine) are now hard-blocked from the network except during explicit model downloads. A damaged model cache can no longer trigger a silent re-download mid-meeting — it fails visibly instead.
- The Faster dictation engine (Parakeet) picks up ~8% quicker decoding from the updated on-device audio stack.
- New in About: "Get beta updates" — opt in to receive the newest builds first. Off by default; everyone else keeps getting stable releases only.
