# Daisy 1.0.7.8 — system audio fixed, smarter auto-stop, low-disk safety, calmer flower

## Fixed

- System audio — the remote side of your meetings — is now reliably saved. Fixed a long-standing bug where the system-audio recording could end up empty (0 bytes) even though the meeting was captured.
- Daisy now warns you mid-meeting if system audio is coming through silent (DRM-protected playback, or a macOS capture glitch), so you find out before the meeting ends rather than after.
- Auto-stop no longer cuts you off mid-sentence. After a calendar meeting's scheduled end, Daisy waits for a stretch of silence before it stops & saves (the 30-second "Keep going" warning still applies).
- The microphone now auto-recovers if it opens on a dead audio device at the very start of a recording, instead of silently capturing nothing.
- Better acoustic-echo cleanup when you record meetings through speakers instead of headphones — far fewer duplicate lines slip into the transcript.
- Starting a new recording right after stopping no longer surfaces a harmless "final pass cancelled" event as an error.

## Changed

- Low disk space is now handled gracefully: when your drive is running low, Daisy automatically records the transcript only (skipping the heavy audio file) and tells you — so a long meeting can't fill your disk. Audio recorded before the switch is kept.
- The recording flower is calmer and clearer: the loader is smooth on ProMotion displays, the centre no longer flickers, the "done" moment is gentler, and it now respects Reduce Motion (System Settings → Accessibility → Display).
- Recording sounds reworked: a clear chime the moment your transcript is ready (in sync with the flower) and a distinct, gentle cue if a recording fails. The pause / resume / stop clicks were removed — they could leak into meetings played through speakers.
- The three recording modes (meeting, dictation, voice note) now read at matched brightness, and dictation's colour is a clearer, brighter lilac.
